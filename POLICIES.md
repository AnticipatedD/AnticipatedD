# Vane-Truth AI Architecture - Sponsorship & Contribution Policies

## 1. Contribution Policy

### Overview
We welcome contributions and sponsorships from individuals, organizations, and entities that align with our mission of advancing AI truth-verification systems and eliminating hallucinations in language models.

### Who We Accept Contributions From

**We gladly accept support from:**
- Individual developers and researchers
- Educational institutions
- Non-profit organizations advancing AI safety and truth verification
- Technology companies committed to ethical AI practices
- Government agencies and public institutions
- Open-source advocates and community members

### Who We Do NOT Accept Contributions From

**We automatically reject contributions from:**

1. **Disinformation & Misinformation Entities**
   - Organizations known for spreading false information
   - Entities engaged in coordinated disinformation campaigns
   - Bad-faith actors deliberately undermining truth-seeking initiatives

2. **Competing Hallucination Technologies**
   - Organizations actively marketing unverified AI systems
   - Entities profiting from deliberate model deception
   - Companies with financial conflicts of interest against truth verification

3. **High-Risk Jurisdictions** (where legally required)
   - Entities under international sanctions
   - Organizations subject to trade restrictions
   - Entities in jurisdictions with compliance concerns

4. **Anonymous/Unverifiable Contributors** (optional enforcement)
   - Contributions without clear source identification
   - Shell organizations with unclear backing
   - Contributors refusing to comply with verification requests

5. **Cryptocurrency-Based Contributions** (if preferred)
   - Crypto tokens or blockchain-based payments
   - Highly speculative assets
   - Contributions that cannot be properly audited

### Contribution Terms

- All contributors must acknowledge our Code of Conduct
- Contributions are voluntary and non-refundable
- No expectation of equity, voting rights, or project control
- Acknowledgment and recognition will be provided on our sponsorship page
- We reserve the right to decline any contribution for any reason
- All contributions subject to applicable laws and regulations

### Process for Rejected Contributions

Rejected contributions will be:
1. Promptly identified and held
2. Declined with a brief explanation (if requested)
3. Returned to the original source within 30 days
4. Documented for compliance purposes

## 3. Reimbursement Expenses Policy

### Eligible Reimbursements

We reimburse contributors and team members for authorized expenses incurred in furtherance of the Vane-Truth AI Architecture project.

### Who Can Request Reimbursement

- Active project contributors
- Team members working on approved initiatives
- Community organizers hosting official project events
- Contractors performing authorized work

### Eligible Expense Categories

#### Travel Expenses
- Transportation to project meetings, conferences, or events
- Accommodation for authorized travel
- Meals during business travel (reasonable amounts)
- Ground transportation (taxi, public transit, rental)
- **Approval Required**: Pre-approval for trips over $500

#### Materials & Supplies
- Hardware for development/testing
- Software licenses for project work
- Office supplies for contributors
- Educational materials and certifications
- **Approval Required**: Pre-approval for purchases over $250

#### Event Costs
- Conference registration fees
- Speaking engagement travel
- Community event organization expenses
- Meetup space rental
- **Approval Required**: Pre-approval required for all event costs

#### Training & Professional Development
- Courses improving project-relevant skills
- Certifications in AI, security, or relevant fields
- Conference attendance and registration
- Workshops and seminars
- **Approval Required**: Pre-approval required for all education expenses

### Reimbursement Process

#### Step 1: Pre-Approval
- Submit expense proposal before incurring costs
- Include description, estimated cost, business justification
- Wait for written approval before proceeding

#### Step 2: Incur Expense
- Conduct transaction maintaining receipts/documentation
- Keep all invoices, receipts, and proof of payment
- Document any business discussions or outcomes

#### Step 3: Submit Claim
- Complete reimbursement form with:
  - Original receipt/invoice
  - Proof of payment
  - Description of expense and project benefit
  - Date and business justification
- Submit within 30 days of expense date

#### Step 4: Review & Approval
- Financial committee reviews for policy compliance
- Contact contributor with questions
- Process approved reimbursements within 15 business days

#### Step 5: Payment
- Reimbursement issued via same method as original payment (when possible)
- Documentation maintained for audit purposes
- Confirmation sent to contributor

### Non-Reimbursable Expenses

❌ Personal, non-business expenses
❌ Alcoholic beverages (except business meals)
❌ Childcare or dependent care
❌ Commuting to regular workplace
❌ Fines, penalties, or parking tickets
❌ Entertainment unrelated to business
❌ Expenses not pre-approved in writing
❌ Claims submitted more than 90 days after expense
❌ Duplicate reimbursements from multiple sources

### Documentation Requirements

- **Original Receipts**: For all expenses over $25
- **Paid Invoices**: For services rendered
- **Proof of Payment**: Credit card statements, bank transfers
- **Business Purpose**: Clear description of project benefit
- **Attendee List**: For events with multiple attendees
- **Business Results**: Outcomes and deliverables from event/training

### Reimbursement Limits

- **Maximum per claim**: $5,000 (higher amounts require board approval)
- **Monthly maximum**: $10,000 per contributor
- **Annual maximum**: Established in annual budget

### Timeline

- **Pre-approval response**: Within 5 business days
- **Reimbursement claim review**: Within 10 business days
- **Payment processing**: Within 15 business days of approval
- **Claim submission deadline**: 90 days from expense date

### Appeal Process

Denied reimbursement requests can be appealed:
1. Submit written appeal with additional documentation
2. Review by senior financial committee
3. Final decision provided within 10 business days
4. Escalation available to project leadership if requested

### Compliance

All reimbursements are:
- Tracked in financial ledger
- Reconciled monthly
- Included in financial reports
- Available for tax and audit purposes
- Subject to applicable laws and regulations
